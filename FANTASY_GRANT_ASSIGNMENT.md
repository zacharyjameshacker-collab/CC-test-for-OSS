### APPLICATION COMPONENTS

**PART I: PROJECT NARRATIVE** (Maximum 15 pages, single-spaced)

**Section 1: Community Need & Urgency (30 points)**
- Describe the specific problems your community faces as a result of the War of the Ring
- Provide quantitative data on the extent of damage and impact
- Explain why this project cannot wait another year for funding
- Describe how the problem disproportionately affects vulnerable populations (elderly hobbits, orphaned children, displaced families, wounded veterans)
- Include stories and quotes from community members describing their challenges

**Scoring Criteria:**
- Problem described with catastrophic urgency and compelling detail (10 points)
- Quantitative data demonstrates significant need (8 points)
- Vulnerability of affected population clearly established (7 points)
- Community voices prominently featured (5 points)

**Section 2: Project Description & Activities (25 points)**
- Provide detailed description of proposed activities
- Explain how activities directly address the identified needs
- Describe the logic connecting activities to expected outcomes
- Include implementation timeline with key milestones
- Describe how project design emerged from community input

**Scoring Criteria:**
- Activities are clearly defined and realistic (8 points)
- Strong logic connecting activities to outcomes (7 points)
- Timeline is detailed and achievable (5 points)
- Community-driven design process evident (5 points)

**Section 3: Organizational Capacity & Management (20 points)**
- Describe your organization's history and relevant experience
- Provide bios of key personnel who will manage this project
- Demonstrate past success completing similar projects on time/budget
- Describe project management approach and accountability systems
- Explain how you will ensure project sustainability after grant ends

**Scoring Criteria:**
- Organization has demonstrated track record (7 points)
- Key staff have relevant, recent experience (6 points)
- Management systems are robust and appropriate (4 points)
- Sustainability plan is realistic and compelling (3 points)

**Section 4: Partnerships & Collaboration (15 points)**
- Identify all partner organizations and their roles
- Describe how partnerships strengthen the project
- Explain coordination mechanisms
- Include letters of commitment from partners
- Demonstrate meaningful collaboration, not just letters of support

**Scoring Criteria:**
- Partners are appropriate and committed (6 points)
- Partnerships demonstrably strengthen project (5 points)
- Coordination plan is clear and realistic (4 points)

**Section 5: Evaluation & Impact (10 points)**
- Describe how you will measure project success
- Provide specific, measurable outcomes with targets
- Explain data collection methods and frequency
- Describe how evaluation findings will inform project adjustments
- Explain how results will be shared with community and foundation

**Scoring Criteria:**
- Outcomes are SMART (specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, time-bound) (4 points)
- Evaluation methods are appropriate and rigorous (3 points)
- Plan includes community feedback and course correction (3 points)

**TOTAL NARRATIVE POINTS: 100**
